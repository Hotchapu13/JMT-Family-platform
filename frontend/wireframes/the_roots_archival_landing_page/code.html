Tier One: The Tribe

Ankole

The Ankole people of Western Uganda represent a lineage defined by their deep spiritual and physical bond with the majestic long-horned cattle. This is an archival exploration of a culture where wealth is measured in horns and legacy is whispered through the lowing of the herd across the savanna.

Regional Roots

Nestled in the Albertine Rift, Western Uganda is a landscape of dramatic geological shifts and verdant peaks. From the snow-capped Rwenzori Mountains to the crater lakes of Queen Elizabeth Park, the soil here holds the memory of empires and the endurance of the land itself.

Tier Three: The Lineage

The JMT Lineage

Tracing the specific branches of the JMT family tree reveals a story of migration, survival, and scholastic achievement. This lineage represents the intersection of traditional Ugandan values and modern global citizenship. Each node in this interactive tree is a curated archive of letters, photographs, and oral histories.
